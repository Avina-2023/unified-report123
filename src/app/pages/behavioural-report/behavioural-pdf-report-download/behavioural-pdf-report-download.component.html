THOUGHT FACTOR focuses on Cognitive factors (Behavioural aspects only) like:
  • Detail-Oriented
  • Critical Thinking
  • Creative Thinking
DETAIL-ORIENTED: This factor refers to the ability to thoroughly accomplish any task by paying attention to even the minutest details. Detail-oriented individuals tend to provide useful information to others on time and double-check the work to ensure accuracy. They are focused on quality and hence strive continuously to improve the process.
CRITICAL THINKING: The ability to critically assess statements and arguments; examine beliefs, assumptions, and opinions and weigh them against facts. They are solely driven by rationality, factual details, and knowledge. They can break the problems into abstract parts, look for an underlying cause and come up with an effective solution.
CREATIVE THINKING: The individual’s ability to think in an unconventional, diverse, and innovative way towards any problem/challenging situation. The need to constantly challenge the status quo. Creative thinkers employ varied approaches to deal with problem situations. They are aware when a new approach is required; they can also come up with a unique solution.
INTERPERSONAL FACTOR focuses on your social competence, which is the ability to handle communication and interactions with others effectively, either individually or socially.
  • Communication Skills
  • Teamwork
  • Positive Attitude
COMMUNICATION SKILLS: The ability to communicate and express with clarity, in a well-organised and logically sequenced way. Individuals with good communication skills are also aware of and receptive to both verbal and non-verbal means of communication. They display the ability to communicate problems in a timely manner.
TEAMWORK: The ability to adapt and work cooperatively with others under varying circumstances. A team player actively participates in the development of team goals and plans, appreciates and acknowledges what the other team members can bring to the table. They are characterised as adaptable, flexible, and able to work accordingly with the team to resolve any challenges and crises.
POSITIVE ATTITUDE: A strong, positive mental state makes one likeable and puts others at ease in any relationship. This factor refers to the individual’s ability to have a frame of mind that looks at any situation in a favourable way.
CORE/PERSONAL FACTOR focuses on a set of core competencies that maps the characteristics of an individual to organisational expectations.
  • Self-Reliance
  • Receptiveness
  • Growth Mind-Set
SELF-RELIANCE: A Self-motivated (driven) individual is one who is completely committed, responsible and strives towards perfection. They take their commitments seriously and tend to be very careful and efficient (timely) in their actions and behaviour. This refers to an individual’s belief and awareness that they can face challenges based on their internal strengths and abilities.
RECEPTIVENESS: The ability to be curious, frank, open and sincere as an individual. Can express one’s views and ideas with openness and sincerity. They are also receptive and open to the ideas and suggestions provided by others.
GROWTH MIND-SET: The individual’s ability to have an optimistic outlook and strong belief in oneself. Is open to learning and constantly work towards growth opportunities. An individual with a growth mind-set tends to view intelligence and knowledge as something that can be developed/improved over time. They show a strong tendency to embrace challenges willingly and use feedback to improve and learn.
EMOTION FACTOR focuses on a set of competencies which maps the individual’s ability to manage emotions and bounce back from any setbacks.
  • Adaptability
  • Emotional Self-Awareness
  • Anxiety Management
  • Empathy
ADAPTABILITY: The ability to change/adjust to different and varying circumstances and people. A person with this ability is flexible and open to new situations and approaches. They display the willingness to learn new ways to achieve work-related tasks and view changes positively.
EMOTIONAL SELF-AWARENESS: The ability to be aware of one’s own emotions/feelings at any given time and their impact on other people and their mood.
ANXIETY MANAGEMENT: Refers to the individual’s ability to handle stressful feelings and anxieties and react appropriately to the current situation.
EMPATHY: The ability to be sensitive to others feelings and concerns, taking into consideration their perspective; appreciating differences in how people feel about things.
