The Four Factors
THOUGHT FACTOR

Constitutes a group of behavioural competencies that focuses on Cognitive factors like Detail Orientation, Critical thinking and Creative thinking. These cognitive competencies are explored from the Behavioural perspective. A person with strong critical thinking is someone who is factual, systematic, evidence-based, and logical in their approach to any task or real-life situation. A person with a creative thinking mindset is more explorative with a very divergent approach to challenges or real-life situations.

CORE / PERSONAL FACTOR

Is focused on the individual SELF. They are a set of competencies that maps the characteristics of an individual to the Organizational expectations. This factor maps individuals to their ability to be self-reliant, have a growth mindset and exhibit openness to various situations/experiences. These are essential characteristics an individual must possess for productive functioning in an organizational context. This factor identifies elements that are present within an ideal organizational citizen. The presence of these traits in the organisational members enables the building of an effective and productive organization.

INTERPERSONAL FACTOR

This is a Social competence, that showcases your ability to handle communication and interactions with others effectively, either individually or socially. Positive social interactions set the stage for other formal and informal interactions. Organizational set-up usually demands teamwork, good interaction skills to enhance work output and productivity.

EMOTION FACTOR

Deals with critical factors that understand the individual’s ability to understand and manage their emotions. It also explores how an individual can effectively channel their emotions in a positive way to communicate effectively, manage relationships and efficiently handle challenging situations. Emotion factors engage with the individual’s ability to adjust to various situations, manage their anxiety, be self-aware and empathetic.
